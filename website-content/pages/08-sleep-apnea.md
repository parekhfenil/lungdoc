## What is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a serious sleep disorder where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. These pauses can last from a few seconds to minutes and may occur 30 times or more per hour.

Left untreated, sleep apnea can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease, diabetes, stroke, and daytime fatigue that affects your quality of life and safety. Our specialists provide comprehensive diagnosis and treatment to help you sleep better and live healthier.

---

## Types of Sleep Apnea

| Type | Description | Prevalence |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)** | Throat muscles relax and block the airway | Most common (90%+) |
| **Central Sleep Apnea (CSA)** | Brain doesn't send proper signals to breathing muscles | Less common |
| **Complex (Mixed) Sleep Apnea** | Combination of obstructive and central | Uncommon |

## Risk Factors

You may be at higher risk if you have:

- Excess weight (BMI > 30)
- Large neck circumference (>17" men, >16" women)
- Age 40+ (though can occur at any age)
- Family history of sleep apnea
- Use of alcohol, sedatives, or tranquilizers
- Smoking
- Nasal congestion or structural issues

## Diagnostic Options

### Home Sleep Apnea Testing
- Convenient testing in your own bed
- Simple device worn overnight
- Accurate for most patients
- Results typically available within days

### In-Lab Sleep Study (Polysomnography)
- Comprehensive overnight monitoring
- Recommended for complex cases
- Measures sleep stages, breathing, oxygen, and more

### Additional Tests
- Oxygen saturation monitoring
- Sleep questionnaires (Epworth Sleepiness Scale)

---

## Treatment Options

### CPAP Therapy (Gold Standard)
- **CPAP** (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) - Constant air pressure
- **BiPAP** - Two pressure levels for inhale/exhale
- Multiple mask styles available
- Ongoing support and adjustment

### Alternatives to CPAP
- **Oral appliances** (dental devices) - For mild to moderate OSA
- **Positional therapy** - For position-dependent sleep apnea
- **Surgery referral** - When appropriate for anatomical issues

### Lifestyle Modifications
- Weight loss (even 10% can significantly improve symptoms)
- Avoiding alcohol and sedatives before bed
- Sleeping on your side
- Regular exercise

## FAQ Section

### How do I know if I have sleep apnea?

Common signs include loud snoring, observed pauses in breathing during sleep, gasping or choking awake, excessive daytime sleepiness despite adequate sleep time, morning headaches, and difficulty concentrating. A sleep study (either at home or in a lab) is needed for diagnosis.

### Can I do a sleep study at home?

Yes! Home sleep apnea testing is convenient and accurate for diagnosing obstructive sleep apnea in most patients. You wear a simple device overnight in your own bed. Some complex cases may still require an in-lab study. We'll help determine which option is right for you.

### Will I have to use CPAP forever?

CPAP is currently the most effective treatment for moderate to severe sleep apnea, and most patients will need ongoing therapy. However, some patients may reduce their CPAP dependence through significant weight loss or, in select cases, surgery. We'll work with you to find sustainable solutions.

### What if I can't tolerate CPAP?

Many CPAP tolerance issues can be resolved with mask fitting adjustments, different mask styles, humidification settings, or pressure adjustments. We work closely with patients to troubleshoot problems. For those who truly cannot use CPAP, alternatives include oral appliances or surgical options.

### Does sleep apnea cause other health problems?

Yes, untreated sleep apnea significantly increases your risk of high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and depression. It also increases the risk of accidents due to daytime drowsiness. Treating sleep apnea can reduce these risks and improve overall health.
