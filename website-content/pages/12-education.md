## Smoking Cessation Resources

**Quitting smoking is the single most important thing you can do for your lung health.**

### Free Resources
- **California Smokers' Helpline:** 1-800-NO-BUTTS (1-800-662-8887)
- **Smokefree.gov** - Federal quit smoking resources
- **This is Quitting** - Text DITCHJUUL to 88709 (for vaping)

### Tips for Quitting
1. Set a quit date
2. Tell friends and family
3. Remove triggers from your environment
4. Consider nicotine replacement therapy
5. Talk to our doctors about prescription options
6. Get support through counseling or quit groups

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**How do I prepare for my pulmonary appointment?**
- Bring a list of all medications (including inhalers)
- Bring your insurance card and ID
- Write down your symptoms and questions
- Bring previous medical records if available
- Avoid smoking before pulmonary function testing

**What should I bring to my first visit?**
- Insurance card
- Photo ID
- List of medications
- Referral (if required by your insurance)
- Previous test results or imaging CDs
