## When to See a Doctor for Shortness of Breath

Shortness of breath (dyspnea) can range from mild to severe, and while occasional breathlessness during exercise is normal, persistent or worsening shortness of breath should be evaluated by a specialist.

### See a pulmonologist if you experience:

- Shortness of breath at rest
- Breathlessness with normal activities (walking, climbing stairs)
- Worsening breathlessness over time
- Shortness of breath when lying down
- Waking up at night unable to catch your breath
- Breathlessness accompanied by wheezing or cough

### Seek emergency care immediately if you have:

- Sudden, severe shortness of breath
- Chest pain
- Bluish lips or fingernails
- Confusion or altered consciousness

## Possible Causes of Shortness of Breath

Shortness of breath can be caused by many conditions, including:

### Lung Conditions
- **Asthma** - Airway inflammation and narrowing
- **COPD/Emphysema** - Chronic lung damage, often from smoking
- **Pulmonary Fibrosis** - Scarring of lung tissue
- **Pulmonary Hypertension** - High blood pressure in lung arteries
- **Pneumonia** - Lung infection
- **Lung Cancer** - Tumors affecting breathing

### Sleep Disorders
- **Sleep Apnea** - Often causes daytime fatigue and shortness of breath with exertion

### Other Causes
- Heart conditions
- Anemia
- Anxiety
- Deconditioning/obesity

**Our job is to find the cause and treat it.**

## Our Evaluation Process

At Inland Pulmonary Specialists, we conduct a thorough evaluation:

1. **Detailed History** - When it started, what makes it worse/better
2. **Physical Examination** - Listening to your lungs and heart
3. **Pulmonary Function Testing** - Measuring lung capacity
4. **Oxygen Assessment** - Checking oxygen levels at rest and with activity
5. **Imaging** - Chest X-ray or CT scan if needed
6. **Additional Tests** - Based on suspected cause
